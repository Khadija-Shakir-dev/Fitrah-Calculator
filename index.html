What is Zakat al-Fitr?
Tap to learn more
Definition

Fitrah (Zakat al-Fitr) is a mandatory charity given by Muslims at the end of Ramadan, before the Eid-ul-Fitr prayer. It is required for every Muslim who has the means, paid on behalf of each family member including children.

Why is Fitrah Important?
  • Purifies the fast from any mistakes or shortcomings during Ramadan.
  • Ensures poor and needy people can celebrate Eid with happiness.
  • Promotes equality and compassion within society.
  • A way of showing gratitude to Allah for completing Ramadan.
Who Should Pay?

Every Muslim with enough food or savings beyond basic needs must pay. The head of the family usually pays for all dependents.

When to Pay?

Pay before the Eid prayer — ideally during the last days of Ramadan so it reaches those in need on time.

How is it Calculated?

A fixed amount per person based on the value of staple food (wheat, dates, barley), multiplied by the number of family members and the rate applicable in your country.
